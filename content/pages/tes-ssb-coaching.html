What is TES?

10+2 Technical Entry Scheme — Army & Navy

TES is a direct officer entry for Class 12 PCM students who have appeared in JEE (Mains). There is no separate UPSC written exam — candidates are shortlisted on JEE rank and Class 12 marks, then called for an SSB Interview. It is one of the fastest routes to a Permanent Commission in the Indian Armed Forces straight after Class 12.

The 5-Day SSB Interview — Day by Day

The SSB is a comprehensive personality assessment spread over 5 days. The Cavalier prepares you for every single stage — nothing is left to chance.

DAY 1

Screening

  • Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR)
  • Picture Perception & Description Test (PPDT)
  • Group Discussion on PPDT story
  • Screening In / Out
DAY 2

Psychology Tests

  • Thematic Apperception Test (TAT)
  • Word Association Test (WAT)
  • Situation Reaction Test (SRT)
  • Self Description (SD)
DAY 3 & 4

GTO Tasks

  • Group Discussion (GD)
  • Group Planning Exercise (GPE)
  • Progressive Group Task (PGT)
  • Half Group Task (HGT)
  • Individual Obstacles & Command Task
  • Final Group Task (FGT)
  • Lecturette
DAY 4 / 5

Personal Interview

  • In-depth PI by interviewing officer
  • Personal background
  • Current affairs & defence GK
  • Motivation & leadership
DAY 5

Conference

  • Board conference
  • Final assessment by all assessors
  • Recommended / Not Recommended
  • Medical for recommended candidates

Frequently Asked Questions — TES & SSB

Everything you need to know before joining The Cavalier for TES SSB coaching.

The 10+2 Technical Entry Scheme (TES) is a direct officer entry for students who have completed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry and Maths (PCM) and appeared in JEE (Mains). Army TES requires a minimum 60% aggregate in PCM; Navy TES requires 70%+. There is no separate written exam — candidates are shortlisted on JEE rank and Class 12 marks and directly called for an SSB Interview. It leads to a Permanent Commission in the Indian Army or Indian Navy.

No — TES has no separate UPSC written exam. Shortlisting is based on your JEE Mains score and Class 12 PCM aggregate. Once shortlisted, you directly appear for a 5-day SSB Interview at a Service Selection Board. This makes TES one of the most direct routes to a Permanent Commission straight after Class 12.

NDA requires passing a UPSC written exam (Maths + GAT) followed by an SSB. TES skips the UPSC written exam entirely — shortlisting is based on JEE Mains rank. NDA trains you at Khadakwasla for 3 years for a degree + commission. TES trains you at a Cadet Training Wing and IMA (Army) or INA (Navy) for 4–5 years for a B.Tech + commission. Both lead to Permanent Commission.

Our 2-week intensive course covers the entire 5-day SSB: Day 1 Screening (OIR + PPDT), Day 2 Psychology Tests (TAT, WAT, SRT, SD), Days 3–4 GTO tasks (PGT, HGT, Command Task, FGT, Lecturette, GD, GPE), and Day 4–5 Personal Interview. We run mock sessions at each stage with written feedback. Study material is included. The course fee is ₹21,000 inclusive of GST.

Ideally, join SSB coaching as soon as you receive your TES call letter — you typically get 4–6 weeks before your reporting date. However, if you want to be fully prepared, joining even before shortlisting (while waiting for results) is a good idea. The SSB tests deeply ingrained personality qualities and OLQs — last-minute cramming does not work. Early preparation is always better.

Each TES notification is a separate attempt. Since TES is notified twice a year (approximately), candidates can appear multiple times as long as they are within the age limit and meet the eligibility criteria. There is no lifetime limit on SSB attempts across different entries — but each TES batch has its own notification and shortlist.
