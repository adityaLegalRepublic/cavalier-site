What is SSB

Services Selection Board Interview

A 5-day selection process at one of India's nine SSBs — the only gateway to become a commissioned officer in the Army, Navy, or Air Force.

🎯

What SSB Assesses

SSB tests 15 Officer Like Qualities (OLQs) grouped into three factors — Social Effectiveness, Dynamic Qualities, and Integration & Stability. These cannot be memorised or rehearsed — they must be genuinely developed and demonstrated consistently over 5 days.

📋

Who Appears at SSB

Any candidate who clears the written exam for NDA, CDS, AFCAT, TES, ACC, NCC Special Entry, or any other commissioned entry is called for SSB. The SSB stage is identical regardless of entry — same 5-day process, same three assessors.

📊

SSB Success Rate in India

The national average SSB selection rate is approximately 1 in 40 candidates. Most failures are not due to lack of capability — they're due to under-preparation for the psychology tests and poor understanding of what is actually being assessed.

🏅

After SSB Recommendation

Recommended candidates proceed to a Medical Board. Those declared fit appear on the Merit List. Final selection depends on merit list rank and vacancy availability. The Cavalier prepares you for the SSB stage — and helps you understand the full pipeline.

The SSB Process

What Happens Over 5 Days

The Cavalier prepares you thoroughly for every single element across all five days.

01
Reporting & Stage 1
  • OIR Test (Verbal + Non-verbal)
  • PPDT (Picture Perception)
  • Group Discussion on story
  • Stage 1 Result
02
Psychology Tests
  • TAT (12 pictures + blank)
  • WAT (60 words, 15 sec each)
  • SRT (60 situations)
  • SDT (Self Description)
03
GTO Day 1
  • Group Discussion (2 rounds)
  • Group Planning Exercise
  • Progressive Group Task
  • Group Obstacle Race
04
GTO Day 2 + PI
  • Half Group Task
  • Individual Obstacles
  • Command Task
  • Lecturette + Personal Interview
05
Conference
  • All three assessors meet
  • Final assessment conference
  • Chest number called = Recommended
  • Medical Board follows

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SSB and The Cavalier's coaching programme.

What is the SSB interview process? ▾
SSB is a 5-day selection process. Day 1 is Stage 1 — OIR test and PPDT. Only those who pass Stage 1 proceed. Stage 2 (Days 2–4) covers Psychology tests (TAT, WAT, SRT, SDT), GTO outdoor tasks (group discussion, planning exercise, group tasks, individual obstacles, command task, lecturette), and Personal Interview with a retired IO. Day 5 is the Conference where all three assessors discuss each candidate.
Who are the faculty at The Cavalier SSB programme? ▾
Three retired officers: Brig Sandeep Mohla SM (Ex-President and Interviewing Officer, 33 SSB Jalandhar), Col DN Sharma (Senior GTO, 19 SSB Allahabad and 17 SSB Bangalore), and Col Vijayant Thakur (Psychologist, 20 SSB Bhopal). All three served at actual SSBs as assessors — not as candidates. This is the single most important difference between The Cavalier and every other SSB coaching institute in Delhi.
How long is the SSB coaching programme? ▾
The SSB Intensive is 2 + 1 weeks. The first two weeks cover all Stage 2 components in depth — psychology test practice, GTO task training, and Personal Interview preparation. The third week is a full 5-day mock SSB with individual feedback from all three officers. A standalone 5-day Mock SSB is also available for candidates who have already done some preparation.
I was screened out / not recommended before. Will this help? ▾
Yes — and The Cavalier specifically addresses repeat attempt candidates. We run a dedicated analysis session to identify which OLQ gaps led to your previous result. For screened-out candidates, we work on Stage 1 (OIR + PPDT) intensively. For not-recommended candidates, we identify whether the gap was in psychology, GTO, or PI — and target that specifically.
Does SSB coaching work or is it just about the person? ▾
SSB assesses Officer Like Qualities — and OLQs can be developed, not just discovered. The Cavalier's approach is not to rehearse answers or fake personality — it is to genuinely develop the qualities being assessed (initiative, social adaptability, effective intelligence, stamina, courage) through structured tasks and feedback from officers who actually know what they look like.
