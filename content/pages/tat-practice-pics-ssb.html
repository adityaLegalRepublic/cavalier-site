PICTURE 01
Four young men in a group discussion at home 🎖 Leadership
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Rahul, a 22-year-old engineering student and class secretary, had called his three closest friends to his house. The college's annual cultural fest needed a fresh theme and they were stuck. Rahul listened patiently as each friend shared an idea, then suggested they combine three concepts into one — "Innovation for the Indian Soldier." His friends loved it. Within a week they presented it to the principal, secured a budget and rallied two hundred volunteers. The fest became the most attended in the college's history. Rahul felt happy that calm listening and quick thinking had united his team.

PICTURE 02
Four students preparing together at a study table 📚 Academic
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Arjun, a 20-year-old NDA aspirant, had organised a group study with three of his classmates two weeks before the maths mid-term. He noticed Priya struggling with calculus. Instead of moving ahead, Arjun stopped, broke down each problem step by step on a notebook and asked the others to help her practise. After three nights of joint study, all four scored above eighty per cent. Their teacher praised the result and asked Arjun to lead the next batch of group studies. Arjun realised that genuine teamwork lifted everyone, not just the strongest student.

PICTURE 03
A young man helping an injured stranger on the roadside 🤝 Helping
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Vikram, a 21-year-old college student, was cycling home when he saw a man lying on the highway, his bike crashed nearby. Without hesitation Vikram dropped his bag, ran to him and checked his breathing. He called the ambulance, applied pressure to a bleeding leg wound using his shirt and waved down passing cars to slow traffic. Within fifteen minutes paramedics arrived. The man recovered fully and later thanked Vikram personally. Vikram believed that any citizen with presence of mind could save a life — uniform or no uniform.

Courage Initiative Determination Stamina
PICTURE 04
A father patiently teaching his son at the study table 👨‍👦 Family
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Sameer, a 28-year-old young father, came home tired from his job to find his son Aarav crying over a tough maths homework. Sameer had a meeting in the morning, but he sat down beside Aarav, smiled and worked through each problem patiently with simple drawings. Within an hour Aarav was solving sums on his own and laughing. The next morning Aarav scored full marks. Sameer realised that small moments of patience built a child's confidence for life. He resolved to spend one focused study hour with his son every evening.

PICTURE 05
A young man giving a presentation to a group 🎖 Leadership
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Karan, a 23-year-old final-year MBA student, had prepared a startup pitch for a national entrepreneurship competition. His three teammates were nervous, so Karan calmly walked them through each slide, encouraged questions and rehearsed twice. PICTURE 06
A girl studying alone in a library 📚 Academic
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Ananya, a 19-year-old college student, had failed her first semester economics paper. She refused to give up. Every evening after classes she walked to the college library, picked up reference books and studied for three focused hours till closing time. PICTURE 07
A young man planting a sapling in his garden 🌱 Civic
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Aditya, a 22-year-old NCC cadet, noticed that his neighbourhood park had lost most of its trees to a recent road-widening project. He spoke to the local councillor, collected fifty saplings from the forest department and gathered ten friends. Every Sunday for a month they planted trees in empty plots and watered them daily. Within two years the area became visibly cooler and birds returned. Aditya felt that one young person with a clear plan could repair what many had ignored. Three young volunteers cleaning a park together 🌱 Civic
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Manav, a 21-year-old engineering student, was disturbed to see his city park covered in plastic waste. He posted in his society's WhatsApp group and gathered Ria and Ravi, two friends. PICTURE 09
A young man repairing his bicycle near a lake 🔧 Self-Reliance
✦ Cavalier Expert Story

Rohan, a 20-year-old college student, was on a long solo cycling trip to the hills when his rear wheel chain broke beside a remote lake. With no mechanic for kilometres, he stayed calm, opened his small tool kit and watched a YouTube tutorial he had downloaded earlier. Within forty minutes he had cleaned, refitted and re-tensioned the chain. He reached his guesthouse before nightfall. Rohan felt proud that careful preparation — a tool kit, downloaded videos, a spare chain link — had turned an emergency into a small adventure he would remember forever.

TAT Golden Rules

Do's and Don'ts in TAT

Every story you write reveals your personality to the assessing psychologist. Follow these rules to keep your hero positive and full of Officer Like Qualities.

✔ Do's — Always Follow
Begin with a clear hero close to your own age
Build a clear start, middle and positive end
Show the hero taking active steps — never passive
Include realistic details — place, time, names
Demonstrate OLQs — initiative, courage, planning
Wrap up with a concrete, achievable outcome
✘ Don'ts — Strictly Avoid
No "Once upon a time" or fairy-tale openings
Avoid passive heroes who only watch others act
No tragic, unresolved or pessimistic endings
Avoid supernatural, fantasy or unrealistic plots
Do not dwell on romance, crime or politics
Never leave the story hanging without an outcome
