What is AFCAT

Air Force Common Admission Test

AFCAT is the Indian Air Force's officer selection exam, conducted twice a year. It is the gateway to all three IAF officer branches — Flying, Technical, and Non-Technical.

✈️

Three IAF Branches

AFCAT selects officers for Flying Branch (pilots, navigators), Ground Duty Technical (aeronautical engineering), and Ground Duty Non-Technical (administration, logistics, accounts, education, meteorology, ATC). One exam, three distinct career paths.

🎓

Who Can Apply

Any graduate can apply for Non-Technical. Engineering graduates apply for Technical Branch. Flying Branch requires 60% in 10+2 with Physics and Maths plus a degree. Age: 20–24 years for Flying, 20–26 for Ground Duty. Both men and women are eligible.

📅

Exam Schedule

AFCAT-I: Forms available December, exam in February. AFCAT-II: Forms available June, exam in August. Conducted online. Shortlisted candidates appear for AFSB (Air Force Selection Board) — the Air Force equivalent of SSB.

🌟

NCC Special Entry

NCC 'C' Certificate holders with Air Wing qualification can apply for Flying Branch through NCC Special Entry — without appearing in the AFCAT written exam. Direct shortlisting for AFSB. The Cavalier prepares candidates for this entry as well.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about AFCAT and The Cavalier's coaching programme.

What is the AFCAT exam? ▾
AFCAT (Air Force Common Admission Test) is conducted twice a year by the Indian Air Force to select officers for Flying Branch, Ground Duty Technical, and Ground Duty Non-Technical branches. It is a 2-hour online exam with 100 MCQ questions — English (30%), Numerical Ability (15%), Reasoning & Military Aptitude (30%), and General Awareness (25%). Each question carries 3 marks with -1 negative marking.
Who can apply for AFCAT? ▾
Any graduate with 60% aggregate can apply for Ground Duty Non-Technical. Engineering graduates apply for Technical Branch. Flying Branch requires 60% in 10+2 with Physics and Maths plus a degree. Age limit is 20–24 for Flying Branch and 20–26 for Ground Duty branches. Both men and women are eligible for all branches.
How long is AFCAT coaching at The Cavalier? ▾
The Cavalier's AFCAT coaching is 3 months, covering all four sections — English, Numerical Ability, Reasoning & Military Aptitude, and General Awareness. Weekly current affairs sessions and regular mock tests are included throughout the programme. Fees: ₹28,000 + GST.
What happens after clearing AFCAT written? ▾
Candidates who clear AFCAT written are called for AFSB (Air Force Selection Board) — the Air Force equivalent of SSB. AFSB is a 5-day selection process assessing Officer Like Qualities through psychology tests, GTO tasks, and Personal Interview. The Cavalier's SSB faculty prepares AFCAT candidates for AFSB as well.
What is the difference between AFCAT-I and AFCAT-II? ▾
There is no difference in the exam structure. AFCAT-I has notification in October/November, forms in December, and exam in February. AFCAT-II has notification in April/May, forms in June, and exam in August. Both offer the same vacancies across Flying, Technical, and Non-Technical branches.
Is hostel facility available at The Cavalier? ▾
