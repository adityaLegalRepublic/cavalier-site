About Us

The Cavalier — Where Officers Are Made

North India's first dedicated SSB coaching academy. Founded 2001. Run by serving and retired Armed Forces officers.

The Cavalier was founded in 2001 by retired officers of the Indian Armed Forces — at a time when the Service Selection Board (SSB) was a closed, opaque world to most aspirants and structured SSB coaching simply did not exist in North India. Today, more than two decades later, we remain what we were on day one: an academy run by officers, for officers-in-the-making.

Our Story

In 2001, the SSB was a near-mythical filter — feared by aspirants, misunderstood by parents, and serviced by no formal coaching ecosystem in North India. The few institutes that did exist focused on the written exam; the SSB was something candidates were expected to face cold, on instinct, with whatever guidance they could scrape together from family or friends in uniform.

A small group of retired Armed Forces officers — men who had themselves served as Interviewing Officers, GTOs and Psychologists at India's Selection Boards — saw the gap and decided to fill it. They had watched too many genuinely deserving candidates fail the SSB simply because no one had explained to them what the assessors were actually looking for. They had the institutional knowledge, the time after retirement, and the conviction that this should be taught properly.

That conviction became The Cavalier — North India's first dedicated SSB coaching academy, opened in Rajouri Garden, Delhi in 2001. The name was chosen deliberately: the cavalier in military tradition is the mounted officer who leads from the front, mobile, decisive, courageous. It is what we wanted our students to become.

We didn't set out to build a tutoring business. We set out to build officers. The written exam is just the gate; the SSB is where India decides whether you have what it takes to lead troops in battle. That cannot be taught from a book. It has to be taught by someone who has sat across the table and made the call themselves.

In the years since, we've added written exam coaching for NDA, CDS, AFCAT, CAPF and Territorial Army — but the SSB programme remains our soul. Our SSB faculty are not actors playing the part of assessors; they are the people who, until very recently, were the assessors. There is no shortcut to that experience.

Milestones

2001
The Cavalier is founded

Founded in Rajouri Garden, Delhi by retired Armed Forces officers as North India's first dedicated SSB coaching academy.

2003
First batch of officer recommendations

Early students convert in their first or second SSB attempt, validating the assessor-led training model.

2008
Written exam coaching launched

In response to repeated student requests, full NDA and CDS written-exam coaching is added — taught by subject experts, not generalist tutors.

2012
5-acre GTO ground commissioned

Dedicated outdoor GTO ground built to actual Selection Board specifications — Progressive Group Task, Half Group Task, Snake Race, Command Task, and the full obstacle course.

2018
AFCAT, CAPF and TA streams added

Expansion into Air Force, paramilitary and Territorial Army officer-entry exam preparation.

2022
First women NDA cadets coached

Following the Supreme Court's 2021 ruling opening NDA to women, The Cavalier coaches its first batch of female aspirants — many subsequently commissioned through both NDA and CDS routes.

2026
25th year

A quarter-century of preparing officers for the Indian Army, Navy and Air Force — and still run on the same principle as on day one.

How We Teach

Three principles have stayed constant since 2001:

  1. Officers teach officers. Our SSB faculty are former assessors at India's Selection Boards. Our written faculty are subject specialists who have lived with these question patterns for years. There is no junior staff or freshly-minted graduate fronting a class.
  2. The ground matters. SSB's Group Testing Officer phase cannot be simulated in a classroom. We built — and maintain — a 5-acre GTO ground in Delhi with the actual obstacle dimensions, the full task suite, and the same scoring rigour. Our students arrive at the real SSB having already run the playbook.
  3. Honesty over flattery. The SSB is a 5-day high-stakes assessment. Our mock SSB results are reported honestly — a candidate who isn't ready hears it, with specific feedback on what to fix. We'd rather lose a student than send them to the actual board unprepared.
