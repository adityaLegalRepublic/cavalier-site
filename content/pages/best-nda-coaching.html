What is NDA

National Defence Academy Exam

The NDA is one of India's most prestigious entrance exams, conducted twice a year by UPSC. It is the gateway to the Army, Navy, and Air Force for students straight out of Class 12.

🎖️

What NDA Offers

A 3-year residential training programme at the National Defence Academy, Khadakwasla, Pune — followed by respective service academies (IMA, INA, AFA). You graduate as a commissioned officer in the Indian Armed Forces.

📋

Who Can Apply

Class 11 and 12 students (appearing or passed). Physics and Mathematics are mandatory for Army/Navy/Air Force. Age: 16.5 to 19.5 years. Both male and female students are eligible for respective entries.

📅

Exam Schedule

UPSC conducts NDA twice a year — NDA I (notification Jan, exam April) and NDA II (notification May, exam Sept). Written exam is followed by SSB interview at one of the Selection Boards across India.

🏅

Career Trajectory

NDA entry leads to a lifetime career as an officer — Lieutenant to General/Admiral/Air Chief Marshal. Salary from Day 1 of training, pension, prestige, and leadership at the highest levels of national service.

Exam Pattern

NDA Written Exam Structure

Two papers totalling 900 marks. Both are MCQ-based. Negative marking applies.

Paper I — Mathematics

300 Marks · 120 Questions
Duration2.5 Hours
Marks per Q+2.5
Negative−0.83
Key TopicsAlgebra, Trig, Calc, Stats
LevelClass 11–12 CBSE

Paper II — GAT

600 Marks · 150 Questions
Duration2.5 Hours
Marks per Q+4
Negative−1.33
SubjectsEnglish, GK, Science
LevelClass 10–12 + Current Affairs

SSB Interview

900 Marks · 5 Days
Stage 1Screening (OIR + PPDT)
Stage 2GTO Tasks
Stage 2Psychology Tests
Stage 2Personal Interview
FinalConference Board
Marking Scheme: Both papers use negative marking. A wrong answer in Paper I deducts 0.83 marks; in Paper II it deducts 1.33 marks. Unattempted questions carry zero marks. Plan your attempt strategy carefully — accuracy over quantity.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about NDA and The Cavalier's coaching programme.

What is the NDA exam pattern in 2025–26? ▾
NDA has two papers: Paper I Mathematics (300 marks, 120 questions, 2.5 hours) and Paper II GAT (600 marks, 150 questions, 2.5 hours). Total written marks: 900. SSB interview also carries 900 marks. Negative marking applies in both papers. Final merit list is based on combined written + SSB score.
Can Class 11 students apply for NDA? ▾
Yes. UPSC allows students appearing in Class 12 to apply for NDA. You can start your NDA preparation from Class 11 — which is exactly what The Cavalier recommends. Starting early gives you two full NDA attempts while still in school.
Is Mathematics mandatory for NDA? ▾
Mathematics is mandatory at Class 12 level for Army, Naval, and Air Force wings of NDA. Students with Arts or Commerce background can only apply for the Army wing (without Maths), but they cannot appear for Paper I and forfeit 300 marks. To maximise your score and all entry options, Maths is highly recommended.
How long is The Cavalier's NDA coaching? ▾
We offer a 3-month and 5-month NDA Foundation batch for Class 12 students, a 2-year NDA Foundation course for students after Class 10, and a 45-day NDA Crash Course before each NDA exam (April and September). All batches include both written preparation and SSB coaching within the same programme.
Does The Cavalier also teach SSB as part of NDA coaching? ▾
Yes — completely. SSB preparation is integrated into every NDA batch. WAT, TAT, SRT, GTO task training, PI preparation, and psychological test practice are all part of the coaching. You do not need a separate SSB coaching institute if you are enrolled at The Cavalier for NDA.
How is The Cavalier different from online coaching? ▾
Online coaching can supplement but cannot replace real SSB preparation, which is experiential — group discussions, outdoor tasks, body language, personal interviews. The Cavalier provides in-person mock SSBs, GTO task simulations, and direct PI feedback from officers who have themselves cleared SSBs. This cannot be replicated on a screen.
