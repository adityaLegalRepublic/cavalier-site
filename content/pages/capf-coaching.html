📋 About CAPF AC Exam

What is the CAPF AC Exam?

The Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) Assistant Commandant exam is conducted by UPSC to recruit officers for India's five central para-military forces. It is one of the most sought-after defence service entries for graduates across India.

The selection process consists of a written exam, Physical Efficiency Test (PET), medical examination, and a personality test (interview). The Cavalier offers specialised coaching for the CAPF written exam with special emphasis on English and Essay writing for Paper II.

✓ Eligibility Criteria

Who Can Apply for CAPF AC?

  • 🎓
    Educational Qualification Graduate Degree in any stream from a recognised university. Final year students appearing in their final exams are also eligible to apply.
  • 📅
    Age Limit 20 to 25 years as on 1st August of the exam year. Age relaxation for SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), and ex-servicemen as per UPSC norms.
  • 🏴
    Nationality Must be a citizen of India. Candidates from Nepal, Bhutan, and specific migrant categories may also be eligible per UPSC guidelines.
  • 💪
    Physical Standards Must meet prescribed height, weight, and chest measurements. Medical fitness per CAPF standards. Physical Efficiency Test (PET) is mandatory after written exam.

🔄 Selection Process

CAPF AC Selection Stages

  • 📝
    Stage 1 — Written ExaminationPaper I (250 marks) + Paper II (200 marks). Conducted by UPSC.
  • 🏃
    Stage 2 — Physical Efficiency Test (PET)100m race, long jump, shot put for men. 100m race and long jump for women. Qualifying only.
  • 🏥
    Stage 3 — Medical ExaminationComprehensive medical board verifying fitness against CAPF standards. LASIK permitted under conditions.
  • 🎤
    Stage 4 — Personality Test / InterviewUPSC board assesses leadership, communication, and personality. Carries 150 marks.

Medical Standards — Key Points

Eyesight: 6/6 or 6/9 better eye; 6/9 or 6/12 worse eye. Near vision N6/N9 corrected.
Refractive Error: Myopia up to −4D; Hypermetropia up to +4D (including cylinder).
LASIK: Allowed if 18–35 yrs, axial 21–26 mm, corneal thickness ≥425 µm, error ≤6D, 6+ months post-op.
Ears: No deafness, discharge, tinnitus, or vertigo.
Nose: No DNS, chronic sinusitis, or rhinitis.
Teeth: Enough sound teeth; no severe gum disease.
General: No TB, diabetes, hypertension, asthma, heart disease, knock knee, flat foot, hernia, or old bad fractures.
Male: Both testicles must be normal in scrotum.
